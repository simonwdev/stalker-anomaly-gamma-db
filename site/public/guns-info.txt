WEAPON'S STAT CARD: WHAT DO THEY MEAN - made by @Jane Blood
Accuracy: Measures the amplitude of the weapon's Aiming down sights' aim cone. At 0%, it means the gun has a 1.5° wide spread cone, at 100%, it has a 0° cone.
Handling: It literally means nothing.
Fire rate: Is self explanatory enough that you SHOULD know what it is without me telling you what it is.
Muzzle velocity: Currently, this stat is the result of a formula ((Weapon base bullet speed) x (First ammo listed for the gun's bullet speed modifier)) x 0.7. See the explanation for the damage stat for more info. The 0.7x modifier on the speed affects only the display text and has no impact on the bullet's actual velocity.
Reliability: Relates to the chance of any part of the weapon to take condition damage upon firing. See !reliability in ⁠🤖bot-commands .
Recoil control: Currently, it's the result of a very long and arbitrary formula that doesn't reflect how recoil works in the slightest due to how random it actually is, you should steer clear of this stat.

WEAPONS UPGRADES: HOW DO THEY WORK
Handling: Reduces inaccuracy modifiers that are present ONLY when not aiming down sights. These include movement and stance (standing/crouched/prone).
Reliability: Reduces chance of any part of the weapon to take condition damage upon firing. For a in-depth explanation, follow the post below this one.
Accuracy: Tightens the dispersion cone regardless of firing from the hip or aiming down sights. These aren't as meaningful as you might think because a 0% accuracy gun has a 1.5° dispersion cone.
Flatness: Increases bullet speed and also improves accuracy by a tiny amount. Damage drop-off is unaffected by these upgrades.
Recoil: Decreases the camera climb when shooting. However, it does not reduce the camera shake.
Fire rate: Increases RPM by a flat amount instead of being a multiplicative bonus. This bonus is usually the same number as what's displayed on the upgrade's info. i.e.: a 60% upgrade would be +60 RPM.

WEAPON RELIABILITY: HOW DOES IT ACTUALLY WORK - trigger warning: ALGEBRA - made by @Jane Blood
It's the result of a function: f(x) = 10000 * (1.0 - x - 0.99),  the 10000x is for display and x being the weapon's condition_shot_dec field, check its .ltx file to get this number, or you could solve the equation. Remember this value as rel.

When you fire your weapon, if the item's general condition (hint: it's hidden, but locked at 83%) is below a given threshold (by default, this threshold is 85% in GAMMA), you roll a dice that's the result of this formula: (85 - (0.83 * 100)) * rel * 2000.
The resulting number is then compared to a random number between 0 and 1000 (generated on every shot), and if this number is lower than the result of the formula, a part of the weapon will take damage.

For example, a gun that has 93% Reliability would mean that it has:
93 = 10000 * (1.0 - x - 0.99)
93 = 10000 - 10000x - 9900
93 = -10000x + 100
93 - 100 = -10000x
-7 = 10000x
0.0007 = x = condition_shot_dec = rel

This would then mean that your chance for a part to take damage is:
(85 - 83) * rel * 2000
2 * 0.0007 * 2000
2.8

Which is compared to a random number between 0 and 1000, and if this randomly generated number is LOWER than this example's 2.8, a random part will take damage.

Do note:
The part chosen to take damage is completely random and does NOT depend on any factor.
The damage a part takes is a random value between 5% and 12%, which also does NOT depend on any factor.
